The For/Against ratio of a party is the division between the plausibility of the party as a good government and the plausibility of the party as a bad one.

The belief toward a party A is the sum of the degrees of support of all the subsets of A. This gives the degree of firmness of the arguments toward A.

The plausibility of a party A is the sum of the degrees of support of all the hypothesis which intersect A.

Hence an hypothesis is as less plausible that his contrary is credible.
Plausibility(A) = 1- Belief(~A).
